Intellectual Property Rights

Overview

Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) are legal protections for creations like inventions, designs, trademarks, and copyrights. They provide creators with exclusive rights to use, produce, and sell their work, preventing unauthorized use by others. IPR promotes innovation by rewarding creators with control over their creations. Common types include patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets. Proper management of IPR helps businesses safeguard their competitive edge and monetize their innovations.
